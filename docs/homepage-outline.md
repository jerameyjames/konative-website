# Konative Homepage Outline

## Purpose
This document turns the credibility-first strategy in `docs/DESIGN-DOC.md` and the offer in `docs/phase-one-offer.md` into a concrete homepage information architecture and supporting page map.

The homepage has one job: help a serious buyer understand what Konative does, why it matters now, and how to take the next step.

## Primary Conversion Goal
Drive qualified prospects to the CTA:

**Request a Project Readiness Review**

## Secondary Conversion Goal
Give investors, partners, and referred prospects enough trust and clarity that they will continue the conversation rather than bounce.

## Homepage Narrative Arc
1. **This market is urgent**
2. **Traditional paths are too slow and fragmented**
3. **Konative is the development partner that brings the moving parts together**
4. **The first engagement produces decision-grade clarity**
5. **The next step is a Project Readiness Review**

## Homepage Section Order

## Section 1: Hero
**Job:** Establish the company, the buyer, the urgency, and the CTA in one screen.

**Content:**
- Headline focused on moving from land and capital to a credible modular data center path
- Supporting line explaining Konative's development-readiness role
- Primary CTA: `Request a Project Readiness Review`
- Optional secondary trust cue: Canada / rural / Indigenous / modular specialization

**Must answer immediately:**
- What is Konative?
- Who is it for?
- Why now?
- What should I do next?

## Section 2: Why This Window Matters
**Job:** Make urgency feel real, not marketing-driven.

**Content:**
- Capital is available
- Land is available
- Power and supply-chain windows are constrained
- Delayed commitments push projects out 6-12 months or more

**Tone:** sober and factual

## Section 3: The Status Quo Is Fragmented
**Job:** Name the buyer's pain clearly enough that they feel seen.

**Content:**
- Traditional data center timelines are too slow
- Teams are piecing together specialists
- Nobody is owning the full front-end decision path
- The result is stalled action and avoidable risk

## Section 4: What Konative Does
**Job:** Show that Konative is the quarterback, not just another advisor.

**Content buckets:**
- site and land path
- power path
- cooling and infrastructure
- supply-chain and long-lead coordination
- pro forma and decision framing
- development orchestration

**Presentation format:** grid or capability bands, not a dense paragraph

## Section 5: Who We Serve
**Job:** Qualify the right audience without overexpanding the market story.

**Primary buyers:**
- investment groups
- Indigenous Development Corporations

**Secondary future buyers:**
- adjacent enterprise or infrastructure stakeholders

For launch, primary buyers should dominate the section.

## Section 6: The Phase-One Engagement
**Job:** Explain what the buyer actually gets if they engage Konative.

**Content:**
- Name: `Konative Development Readiness Engagement`
- Decision-grade diligence across site, power, cooling, supply chain, and pro forma
- Typical outputs: risk register, executive readout, go/no-go framing
- Pricing posture: `engagement-based pricing`
- CTA repeated at the end of the section

## Section 7: Trust Layer
**Job:** Build confidence without depending on named case studies.

**Content options:**
- founder/team credentials
- geography/domain expertise
- anonymized sample deliverables or engagement structure
- clear and professional legal/contact posture

**Important:** competence-first trust is acceptable for v1

## Section 8: What Happens Next
**Job:** Reduce ambiguity around the CTA.

**Content:**
- You request a Project Readiness Review
- Konative reviews fit, urgency, and project context
- Qualified projects move into the phase-one engagement discussion

This keeps the CTA from feeling like a black box.

## Section 9: Final CTA Band
**Job:** Close with a strong, clean ask.

**Content:**
- concise restatement of buyer fit
- primary CTA button
- optional direct email fallback

## Section 10: Footer
**Job:** Close professionally and support trust.

**Content:**
- Konative brand
- contact path
- privacy / legal / terms if needed
- any public branding/legal language chosen for launch

## Supporting Page Map

## Page: Home
The main trust and conversion surface.

## Page: What We Do
Expanded explanation of Konative's role, capabilities, and decision-grade engagement.

## Page: Who We Serve
Buyer-specific framing for investment groups and Indigenous Development Corporations.

## Page: Contact / Request a Project Readiness Review
The CTA destination with intake form and expectations.

## Page: Privacy
Required for launch if form capture and analytics are present.

## Page: Legal / Terms / Contact Details
As needed based on final public legal posture.

## Optional Later Pages
- insights or perspective page
- partner ecosystem page
- sample deliverables page
- gated client workspace or knowledge base

These should not block launch.

## Navigation Recommendation
Keep navigation short:

- Home
- What We Do
- Who We Serve
- Request a Project Readiness Review

If the site is one-page for launch, convert these into anchored sections and keep the CTA persistent.

## Homepage Content Priorities
If time is constrained, prioritize in this order:

1. Hero
2. Why this window matters
3. What Konative does
4. Phase-one engagement
5. Trust layer
6. CTA destination
