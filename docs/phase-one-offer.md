# Konative Phase-One Offer

## Status
Draft for review

## Offer Name
**Konative Development Readiness Engagement**

## One-Line Summary
A decision-grade engagement for investment groups and Indigenous Development Corporations that need to move from land + capital to a credible modular data center go/no-go decision before power and supply-chain delays push the project out another 6-12 months.

## Recommended Public CTA
**Request a Project Readiness Review**

This CTA is stronger than a generic "Contact us" and broader than "Request a pro forma." It matches what Konative is actually selling: a serious front-end diligence engagement that results in a decision path.

## Who This Is For
- Investment groups trying to put capital to work in data center infrastructure
- Indigenous Development Corporations with land and development ambition
- Buyers facing urgency around power, turbine sourcing, cooling, siting, and delivery sequencing

## What Problem It Solves
The buyer does not need another vague strategy deck. They need a single partner who can coordinate the early-stage diligence that makes a modular data center project real:

- site viability
- power path and constraints
- cooling and infrastructure implications
- supply-chain critical path
- pro forma assumptions
- key risks and next decisions

Without that, they stay stuck between fragmented consultants, long traditional timelines, and capital that cannot move confidently.

## Commercial Structure
### Recommended pricing
Internally, Konative should anchor this engagement around **$300,000** for a phase-one scope.

### Public pricing posture
Use **engagement-based pricing** on the public site rather than publishing the dollar figure. The public site should emphasize fit, urgency, and decision value; pricing can be discussed during the review conversation once project scope is clearer.

### Recommended structure
- Fixed-fee phase scoped around a single target project or site pathway
- Additional specialist work or expanded geography/site comparison can be priced as change-order or phase-two work

This keeps the public offer concrete while preserving room for scoped complexity.

## Recommended Timeline
**8-10 weeks** from kickoff to executive readout

This is short enough to feel urgent, but long enough to produce something decision-grade instead of superficial.

## What The Client Receives
### Core deliverables
1. **Project framing brief**
   - project objective
   - target capacity assumptions
   - key timeline and capital assumptions

2. **Site and infrastructure diligence memo**
   - site viability considerations
   - power path options and constraints
   - cooling and remote-environment implications
   - key land/infrastructure dependencies

3. **Supply-chain and critical-path review**
   - long-lead items
   - likely sequencing pressure points
   - near-term commitment implications

4. **Risk register**
   - major delivery, regulatory, infrastructure, and timing risks
   - severity and mitigation framing

5. **Decision-grade pro forma**
   - assumptions-based commercial model
   - major cost and timing drivers
   - scenario sensitivity where appropriate

6. **Go / no-go recommendation**
   - whether to proceed
   - what must be true to proceed
   - what must be secured next

7. **Executive readout**
   - stakeholder-ready summary
   - next-phase recommendations

## What Is Included
- Founder-led strategy and orchestration
- Specialist coordination as needed for the phase-one decision package
- Structured review of site, power, cooling, and supply-chain decision factors
- Commercial framing robust enough to support a real next-step conversation

## What Is Not Included
- Full detailed engineering package
- Final permitting execution
- Full procurement management
- Construction management
- Long-term operations platform or client portal

Those can become phase-two or later-scope work once the project clears the readiness gate.

## Client Inputs Required
To move quickly, the client should be prepared to provide:

- target geography or site context
- intended use case and capacity direction
- stakeholder and ownership structure
- timeline urgency
- any known land, utility, or infrastructure constraints
- available financial assumptions or target returns

## Definition of Success
This engagement is successful if, at the end of the phase, the client can answer:

- Is this project credible enough to advance?
- What are the major risks?
- What decisions need to be made now?
- What commitments must be secured to avoid losing time?
- Should we proceed, pause, or re-scope?
