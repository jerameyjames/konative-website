Most "Canada is the next data center frontier" pieces frame the country as a single emerging market. That's wrong. Canada has **117 existing data centers**, **30+ in development**, and roughly **10 GW of announced capacity** — but it operates under four structurally different power regimes, and the federal Sovereign AI Compute Strategy is committing **C$2 billion** with explicit preference for Indigenous-partnered projects.

Here's how we're reading the map at Konative.

## The four provincial regimes

**Quebec — re-opening, on a price.**
After a multi-year pause that was *de facto* a moratorium on crypto and large new DC loads, Hydro-Québec is launching a new large data centre rate (~13¢/kWh, roughly 2× existing large-power rates) in the **second half of 2026**. Microsoft has four active builds in the province. Vantage's QC61 (30 MW) went live January 2026. The hydro is unmatched. The bottleneck is no longer transmission — it's the allocation framework.
*Konative read: Quebec is the right answer if your project is OK with a multi-year competitive process and a higher-than-historical rate. It's the wrong answer if you need to break ground in 12 months.*

**Ontario — biggest market, tightest grid.**
Toronto hosts ~35 facilities and is Canada's densest peering hub (TORIX). Bill 40 (June 2025) gives the Minister of Energy authority to **prioritize DC connection requests**, and IESO forecasts 16 new data centers connecting in the next decade — but transmission near the GTA is effectively full through the late 2020s.
*Konative read: Ontario rewards landholders **outside** the GTA — the I-401 corridor toward Kingston, eastern Ontario, sites near existing 500kV. That's where new substations can be built without a 5-year wait.*

**Alberta — the bring-your-own-power market.**
The only Canadian market where you can credibly co-locate generation with load **behind the meter**. Natural gas + BESS + AESO grid backup. eStruxture's C$750M (US$585M) 90 MW Calgary build and AVAX One's 10 MW HPC project are templates. **30+ AI DC projects** are sitting in the AESO queue as of February 2026.
*Konative read: Fastest path from LOI to powered land in Canada — if you bring your own generation. Calgary metro is the gravity center; the Edmonton corridor is expanding.*

**British Columbia — constrained, but Site C is fresh MW.**
BC will limit 2026 DC interconnections to **300 MW for AI and 100 MW general**, banning new crypto entirely. That cap creates scarcity premium — and Site C Dam (~1.1 GW) is genuinely new clean MW available. Most viable opportunities here will be First Nations-partnered builds in the Peace region.

## The defining vector: First Nations partnerships

The federal C$2B Sovereign AI Compute Strategy explicitly prefers Indigenous-partnered projects. That isn't soft language — it's structural advantage that determines who clears the queue.

Three projects worth watching:

- **Woodland Cree First Nation (Alberta)** — 650 MW Mihta Askiy data centre, Woodland Cree owns 51% with developer Sovereign Digital Infrastructure. Phase 1 startup mid-2027 on natural gas. **Largest announced Indigenous-led DC in North America.**
- **Prophet River First Nation (BC)** — LOI with ABCT Pacific to develop a major DC near Fort St. John, leveraging Site C completion. Prophet River holds majority stake.
- **Upper Nicola Band (BC)** — Community consultation on a $500M proposed AI data center; lease-revenue model.

The federal RFP for sovereign AI compute closes **June 1, 2026**. The applications that will clear are the ones with First Nations partnership structures already in place.
