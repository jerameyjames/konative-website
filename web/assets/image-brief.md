# Hero Photography Direction

## Current placeholders (replace with Konative-specific work)

The PNGs in `assets/` are **temporary public-domain** photographs from Wikimedia Commons (see `assets/sources.md`) chosen for warm color temperature and editorial restraint. They exist so layout and components can ship before final art direction photography is commissioned.

## What the hero imagery should depict
- Premium outdoor living installations with built-in stone elements, purposeful spatial planning, and fabrication detail.
- Natural stone and surface applications in real project contexts (patios, outdoor kitchens, hospitality-grade exterior zones).
- Craftsmanship moments where material, precision, and installation quality are visually obvious.
- Pacific Northwest environmental context: layered ridgelines, evergreen framing, and atmospheric light.
- Composed scenes that imply trusted partnership between brands, reps, and spec-driven project teams.

## Mood
- Warm golden-hour light with soft contrast and believable highlights.
- Natural materials foregrounded: stone, wood, metal, concrete, weathered textures.
- Editorial restraint over glossy promotion; calm, assured, and premium.
- Human presence can be subtle and purposeful (hands at work, collaborative posture), not staged stock smiles.

## Five specific image concepts
1. **Signature outdoor installation, dusk edge-light**  
   A wide architectural view of a custom outdoor living area with integrated stone counters and seating, warm sky tones, and restrained landscaping.
2. **Fabrication close-up, tactile material story**  
   Tight crop of hands refining a stone edge with visible tooling marks, dust, and texture transitions under warm side light.
3. **Spec-and-sample collaboration table**  
   A woman-led team in conversation over material boards, cut samples, and annotated plans in a daylight studio.
4. **Installed surface detail in context**  
   Mid-shot of premium slab surfaces meeting wood and metal finishes in an exterior kitchen zone, emphasizing joinery and quality.
5. **PNW landscape + built environment bridge**  
   A composition where built outdoor elements sit against layered ridges and evergreens, communicating place and long-term durability.

## Color temperature and edit direction
- Target overall white balance in the warm range (approximately 5200K-6200K visual feel).
- Preserve natural material color fidelity; avoid over-saturation or heavy teal/orange cinematic grading.
- Slightly lifted shadows for legibility while keeping depth in blacks.
- Grain should be subtle and filmic, never crunchy or synthetic.
- Avoid cool, sterile, blue-heavy edits unless balancing a clearly warm foreground subject.
